# Brainstorming: Advanced & Interesting Features for SchemeLens

You already have an incredible foundation: **Semantic AI Search**, **Citizen Feedback**, and a **Mathematical Risk Engine**. To make this Community Service Project truly stand out and blow the evaluators away, here are three highly advanced, interesting features we can build next:

---

## Idea 1: The "Am I Eligible?" AI Matchmaker (Highly Recommended)
**The Problem:** Currently, the AI finds *relevant* schemes based on a search, but the citizen still has to read the description to figure out if they actually qualify legally (e.g., age, income, caste).
**The Feature:** We create a **User Profiler**. A citizen enters their details (Age: 22, Income: ₹50,000, Gender: Female). We write a new algorithm that uses our AI to extract the hard requirements from the scheme text and mathematically checks if the user's profile matches the rules!
**Why it's cool:** It turns the system from a "Search Engine" into an "Intelligent Advisor."

## Idea 2: Multilingual Voice Assistant (Accessibility Tech)
**The Problem:** The abstract states this is for rural and semi-urban areas. What if the citizen cannot read or type?
**The Feature:** We add a Microphone button to the web interface. The citizen speaks their problem (e.g., "I need money for seeds"). We use **Speech-to-Text (STT)** to convert their voice to text, feed it to our AI Engine, and then use **Text-to-Speech (TTS)** to read the recommended scheme out loud to them!
**Why it's cool:** This proves your project actually solves real-world accessibility issues for illiterate or rural populations.

## Idea 3: The Government "Bloat" Detector (AI Duplication Checker)
**The Problem:** The government often launches a new scheme that does the exact same thing as an old scheme, wasting millions in administrative costs.
**The Feature:** Since we already built a Vector Database (FAISS), we can write a script that calculates the mathematical distance between all 4,580 schemes. It will automatically detect **Duplicate or Highly Overlapping Schemes** (e.g., two different departments offering the exact same agricultural subsidy) and flag them to the government for consolidation.
**Why it's cool:** This is a brilliant, high-level computer science application of vector embeddings that saves government tax money.
